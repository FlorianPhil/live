1. Leader Onboarding

What being a chapter leader means, what you sign, and your first 30 days.

📋

Start here. This page walks you through what being a chapter leader actually means, what you need to sign, and what to do in your first 30 days.

What it means to be a Chapter Leader

🗓️

Leadership teams serve a 1-year term. Leaders are expected to attend all meetings and support and attend local chapter events in their region.

  • You are the face of the Chamber in your region. Members, sponsors, and the press will look to you first.
  • You run at least one chapter touchpoint per month (event, call, meetup, or roundtable).
  • You are a volunteer, not an employee. We respect your time.
  • You are expected to follow Chamber brand and compliance rules (see Brand Kit and Social Media SOPs).

Leadership Commitment

⚠️

You must sign the Leadership Commitment and Code of Conduct before taking on a leadership role. Read it, sign it, return it to HQ.

By serving as a chapter leader, you commit to the following:

  • Become a formal paying member of the organization.
  • Attend all regularly scheduled meetings, with 1 absence permitted.
  • Support and attend the chapter events in your region.
  • Demonstrate appropriate leadership, reliability, and professionalism in any roles, duties, or obligations you volunteer for within the organization.
🚨

Any board member that does not meet the above commitments could be subject for removal from the organization.

Leadership Code of Conduct

The Cannabis Chamber of Commerce strives to create a more open and inclusive environment for professionals of the cannabis industry and shall not tolerate any forms of aggression, harassment or discrimination. As a leader and member of this organization, you are expected to conduct yourself professionally, ethically, and responsibly, with the understanding that your actions reflect on the reputation of our organization.

Civility and respect

A member shall conduct oneself with civility and respect towards all others.

Rules of order

A member shall observe and abide by the rules of order and shall maintain decorum during meetings.

At all times, including online

A member shall observe and abide by the principles embodied in the Code of Conduct at all times, including when engaged in electronic communications: phone calls, text messages, emails, social media, and social networking sites.

No disparagement or harassment

A member shall not disparage or harass others in any meetings or events associated with the organization. Harassment includes, but is not limited to:

  • Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following of a person
  • Slurs, epithets, name-calling, ridicule, threats, or obscene gestures
  • Displaying inappropriate or offensive images at any organization meeting, event, or space
  • Unwelcome advances
  • Coercing inappropriate sexual or other conduct in exchange for a benefit
  • Retaliation against complainant or witnesses to harassment
  • Advocating for, or encouraging any of the above behavior
No discrimination

A member shall not discriminate against nor use discriminating language on the basis of race, color, ancestry, national origin, religion, sex, disability, age, genetic information, marital status, sexual orientation, gender identity and expression, medical condition, immigration status, physical appearance, military or veteran status, or the status as a victim of domestic violence, assault, or stalking.

No detrimental actions

A member shall not engage in any actions detrimental to The Cannabis Chamber of Commerce.

Fulfill your duties

A volunteer leader shall fulfill their duties as detailed in the Leadership Commitment.

⚖️

Violations may result in removal. Members who violate this Code of Conduct or otherwise break down order may be ejected from any meeting, and may be subject to censure, suspension, or removal from the organization.

Required documents

  • Signed Board Member Commitment and Code of Conduct
  • Active paying membership in the Chamber (see Pitch Kit for tiers)
  • W-9 or equivalent for any reimbursable expenses
  • Headshot and short bio for the Chamber website
  • Local chapter name and region approved by HQ

Your first 30 days

1️⃣

Week 1. Read every page of this toolkit. Sign and submit the Leadership Commitment and Code of Conduct to HQ. Introduce yourself on the global Chapter Leaders channel.

2️⃣

Week 2. Announce your chapter on your personal social channels. Identify 5 founding members to invite personally. Pick a date for your kickoff event.

3️⃣

Week 3. Confirm your kickoff venue or virtual platform. Send the first member outreach email. Log the event in the Chapter Events database.

4️⃣

Week 4. Host your kickoff or finalize the run-of-show. Submit your first monthly check-in to HQ. Add feedback or suggestions to this toolkit.

Who we are

🌱

The Cannabis Chamber of Commerce was created to support the growth and development of businesses that serve the regulated cannabis industry by offering a meaningful trade association that offers resources at the local level and a unified voice on the national stage.

FAQ: common objections

"How is this different from [other cannabis association]?"

Most cannabis associations are lobbying groups or regional event producers. The Chamber is a proper trade association: local regional chapters led by operators, a unified national voice, networking and education programming, and political representation at the regional level. We follow the long history of US chambers of commerce, adapted for this industry.

"What does it actually cost?"

$100 to $500 per month depending on tier, or $1,000 to $5,000 annual. Silver, Gold, or Platinum. See the tier breakdown above.

"I am not an operator, can I still join?"

Yes. The Chamber is built for businesses that serve the regulated cannabis industry, which includes operators, ancillary businesses, investors, consultants, and service providers. The full ecosystem.

"What do I actually get for my money?"

Depends on the tier, but every member gets event access in their region, educational workshops, member directory listing, and local government representation. Gold and Platinum add cross-region visibility, social promotion, and booth access at events in any region.

"Is the Chamber political?"

The Chamber provides political representation and a unified voice for the industry at the regional and national level. We are not a PAC and we do not endorse candidates. Our political work focuses on industry standards, regulatory clarity, and community representation.

4. Social Media SOPs

How to post without getting flagged. Templates, rules, and hashtags.

⚠️

Read this before posting anything on behalf of the Chamber. Cannabis content gets flagged, removed, and shadow-banned on most major platforms. Follow these rules to stay visible.

Posting cadence

  • Minimum: 1 event announcement + 1 recap per chapter event
  • Recommended: 2 to 4 posts per week during event weeks
  • Always tag: @cannabischamber (placeholder handle) so HQ can reshare

Compliance: words to avoid

🚫

Do NOT use these words in captions, hashtags, or images on Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, or LinkedIn.

AvoidUse instead
MarijuanaCannabis
Weed, pot, budCannabis, the plant
Buy, sale, for sale, purchaseLearn more, connect, join
THC, CBD (as product claims)Industry, operators, companies
Get high, stoned, blazedavoid entirely
Dispensary deals, discountsMember benefits, networking
Any price tied to productavoid entirely
💡

Rule of thumb: write as if you are promoting a business organization, not a product. The Chamber is a trade association, not a dispensary.

What you CAN post

  • Chapter events (networking, education, panels)
  • Member spotlights (the person and the business, not the product)
  • Industry news and commentary
  • Advocacy and policy updates
  • Photos from events (people, venues, speakers)
  • Sponsor thank-yous (brand name and logo only, no product shots)

5. Event Playbook

Checklists for running chapter events that deliver value.

📅

Your step-by-step for running chapter events that actually deliver value. Copy the checklists. Don't skip steps.

Event types

🥂

Networking mixer (easiest to run). 2 to 3 hours, venue with drinks, 30 to 80 people. No formal program. Low stress, high member value.

🎤

Educational panel. 90 minutes, 3 to 4 speakers, moderated Q&A, followed by networking. Higher prep, higher ceiling.

🗣️

Roundtable or working session. 60 to 90 minutes, 8 to 15 people, specific industry topic. Best for deep discussion and relationship-building.

💻

Virtual event. 45 to 60 minutes on Zoom, global audience. Fastest to organize, lowest cost.

Pre-event checklist (4 to 6 weeks out)

  • Pick date, format, and topic
  • Confirm venue or virtual platform
  • Log event in the Chamber Events database
  • Confirm speakers or panelists (if applicable)
  • Design event flyer
  • Draft event description and RSVP copy
  • Send first announcement to your member list
  • Post first social announcement
  • Notify HQ so they can amplify

2 weeks out

  • Second social push
  • Direct outreach to target attendees
  • Confirm speaker logistics (travel, bios, headshots)
  • Confirm catering or venue details
  • Create attendee list / RSVP tracker

1 week out

  • Final reminder email to RSVPs
  • Final social push with "last chance to RSVP" angle
  • Confirm run-of-show with venue and speakers
  • Print name badges (if in-person)
  • Assign a photographer

Day-of checklist

⏰

Arrive early. Always.

  • Arrive 60 to 90 minutes early
  • Set up signage and brand materials
  • Test AV (mics, slides, speakers)
  • Brief speakers on timing and format
  • Greet first attendees personally
  • Take photos: venue, signage, crowd, speakers, candid networking
  • Capture 1 to 2 short video clips for social
  • Open with a 2-minute Chamber intro
  • Close with next event announcement and CTA to join

Post-event checklist (within 48 hours)

  • Upload photos to shared drive
  • Post event recap on social
  • Send thank-you email to attendees and speakers
  • Send sponsor thank-you (if applicable)
  • Update Chamber Events database with final count and notes
  • Send short recap to HQ (1 paragraph + 3 photos)
  • Note lessons learned for the next event
